# 05.1 — Report finding

Direct quote from `deep-research-report-3.md`, "Detailed findings":

> "Slint bridge callbacks (`start-gstpop-service`, `start-migration-runtime-service`,
> etc.) call into `GstPopServiceBridge.startGstPopServiceHost`,
> `MigrationRuntimeServiceBridge.startMigrationRuntimeHost`, and similar
> statics. There is no abstraction layer between the UI callback and the JNI
> bridge — and on the Rust side, `BACKEND: Lazy<RwLock<Arc<dyn MediaBackend>>>`
> is a process-global mutable singleton. The combination makes it impossible
> to instantiate a 'test backend' or run two backends in parallel for A/B
> testing."

Proposed mitigation (high priority):

> "**Introduce a typed Kotlin `RuntimeBridge` interface and an explicit
> composition root.** On the Android side, the activity should depend on
> `RuntimeBridge`, not on `GstPopServiceBridge`. On the Rust side, replace
> the static `BACKEND` with a `BackendRegistry` trait owned by a small
> `App`-context struct that is constructed once during JNI bootstrap."

## Why "high priority"

This is the prerequisite for:

- **Step 10** (Android-side tests) — the controller needs a fake
  `RuntimeBridge`.
- **Step 12** (performance pass) — the benchmark harness wants a synthetic
  backend.
- **Step 06** (secret-store split) — the new `SecretStore` trait fits into
  the same `App` context.

Without composition-root infrastructure these later steps are not testable in
JVM.

## Why "Medium effort" instead of "Low"

The Kotlin side is small (~150 LOC across 4 classes). The Rust side is
larger because the `BACKEND` global is touched from many places in
`src/lib.rs`; replacing each callsite with a borrow of the new `App`
context is mechanical but high-volume. Land the Rust side as its own commit
(see 05.12 for rollback).
